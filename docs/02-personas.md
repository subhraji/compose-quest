# User Personas

## P1 — Maya, the Absolute Beginner *(primary)*

- **Age/role:** 19, CS student. Knows a little Java/Python. Never built an Android app.
- **Goal:** Build her first real Android screen and not feel lost.
- **Pains:** Official docs feel like a wall of jargon. Tutorials assume she knows things she doesn't. She gives up when something doesn't work and there's no one to ask.
- **What she needs from Compose Quest:**
  - A clear path so she always knows what's next.
  - Plain-language explanations (and a "like I'm 10" button for when she's truly stuck).
  - To *see* abstract ideas, not just read them.
  - Instant help the moment she's confused.
- **Win condition:** "I built a tappable counter and I actually understand why it works."

## P2 — Sam, the Rusty Returner *(secondary)*

- **Age/role:** 31, backend dev who dabbled in Android XML years ago.
- **Goal:** Get productive in Compose fast; fill specific gaps (state, navigation, ViewModel).
- **Pains:** Knows enough to be dangerous; doesn't want baby-talk for *everything*, but needs the new mental models.
- **What he needs:** Skip-ahead via the roadmap, strong analogies for the new declarative model, quick quizzes to confirm understanding, a mentor to sanity-check his code.
- **Win condition:** "Now I get why recomposition isn't scary."

## P3 — Priya, the Curious Explorer *(secondary)*

- **Age/role:** 26, web/React dev curious about native Android.
- **Goal:** Understand how Compose compares to what she knows (declarative, state, hooks-like patterns).
- **Pains:** Doesn't want to set up Android Studio just to learn the concepts.
- **What she needs:** A zero-setup, in-browser way to grasp the model and play with code. Analogies that bridge from React mental models.
- **Win condition:** "I can reason about Compose without installing anything."

## P4 — Mr. Okafor, the Mentor/Teacher *(influencer)*

- **Age/role:** 40, bootcamp instructor.
- **Goal:** Point students to something that actually makes Compose click.
- **What he needs:** A trustworthy, structured, beginner-proof resource with built-in practice and an AI tutor that won't mislead.
- **Win condition:** "I can assign a stage and trust they'll get it."

---

### Design implications

| Persona | Implication |
|---|---|
| Maya (primary) | Default everything to the gentlest path; make help unmissable; reward every step. |
| Sam | Let the roadmap support skipping; keep analogies first-class, not childish-only. |
| Priya | Zero-setup playground is essential; offer cross-paradigm analogies via the mentor. |
| Mr. Okafor | Accuracy + structure + safe AI tone; clear progress to track. |
